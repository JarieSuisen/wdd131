Hi! I'm so glad you came to visit for a bit. I'd like to share something with you, an amazing gift that is available to everyone! It is called the Plan of Salvation. It was prepared even before the creation of the world, for the potential benefit of every single person.
Let's start with some simple questions:

  • What comes after this life?
  • Would you like to be with your loved ones again at that time?
  • Do those that have suffered so much deserve so much more than they had in life?

If you have wondered things like that, then hopefully this Plan will bring hope to you.

"Okay, that sounds interesting. But why should I care about this? It sounds like just another hopeful afterlife."

Hey, I get it. There are a LOT of ideas out there about what might be the truth. If you would like to learn some more about why you might want to learn more, I share more details about the Plan of Salvation and how it rewards everyone on the page linked below:

Reward Descriptions (Okay, maybe that sounds like a sweestakes, but please take a look anyways!)

"So, what do I need to do?"

Good question! And the simple answer is "just listen to God and follow His guidance". However, since a critical part of the plan is to be able to choose for yourself He requires us to learn and teach each other. No person can obtain the greatest rewards offered on their own, and that is very intentional. Godhood is not suitable for the selfish and greedy. (Really, that would be a terrifying prospect! There are plenty of fantasy stories about evil god-like entities, I don't think anybody actually wants that to be real. People that make harmful choices are enough of a challenge.) So, here is a page where I share some of the requirements that are in place to attain the highest rewards, as we understand them.
Although it can feel confounding, the Plan has a fair amount of intentional vagueness in some regards because real choices require a real chance of choosing differently. If you had all the answers and it was completely clearly laid out, what person would choose against it? Paradoxical as it may sound, it isn't the desire for anyone to choose against the Plan and fail. Rather, it is the desire and hope that each person will choose it despite other options.
